full app redesign Lighthouse UI In the summer of 2017 I interviewed at Lighthouse and was given a simple task: Go through the product, make note of UX issues, and design any necessary solutions.

Position
UX Interviewee
Team
Solo
When
September 2017
Time
3.5 hrs

Initial discovery

Users rely on Lighthouse to manage 1 on 1 meetings with their team members, and the core of Lighthouse is built on these key experiences:

  1. users need to be able to navigate their 1 on 1s with their teams easily
  2. weekly action items need to be surfaced without being distracting
  3. users want to edit their meeting notes securely and easily

Designing & implementation

After some core issues were identified, I began wireframing some basic workflows, layouts, and UI tweaks. I decided to focus on visual tweaks to enhance experience and give the UI a slightly more modern touch:

  • lighten up the color palette to resolve any distractions on the page
  • modify the typography to help the readability of meeting notes and agenda items
  • clean up font declarations to resolve hierarchy issues
  • refresh the text-editing components to be more *modern

Post interview

In late September I was given an offer to join the Lighthouse team, which happened to land on my birthday, and roughly 2 weeks later the redesign I worked on deployed. The redesign introduced tighter headers, a lighter overall theme, and more consistent typography.

Results and metrics

Over the course of 6 months I launched 2 redesigns for Lighthouse: the first when I initially onboarded, and the second 6 months later which revamped the entire UI and overall UX - consisting of:

  • a typeface change making it easier to read and take extensive notes (from Montserrat to Open Sans)
  • a refreshed color palette that moved away from heavy greens
  • more cohesive IA, helping users access their information easily

The redesign was very well received by users, and resulted in a decrease in churn, going from 6.45% to 2.75%!

full feature life-cycle design Manager Score

An app that allows leaders to be given proper insights and analytics into their performance, by their team members.

Insights are candid, properly analyzed, and most importantly actionable.

Position
UX Designer | Font-end Engineer
Team
3 people
When
June 2017
Time
2 months

Task

Design, build, and iterate:

  • Marketing pages and assets (iconography, illustrations, etc...)
  • Signup and onboarding flows
  • Survey UI components
  • Data and analytics views
  • Data Exports

Assumption

Managers need actionable insights from their team and team members need to answers survey questions about their manager's performance. Given this assumption, a basic workflow was ideated on:

  1. managers need to request feedback from their team members
  2. team members have to be confident that survey answers will remain anonymous
  3. surveys need to be accessed easily by team members
  4. all collected data need to have both analytics and media content in order to provide value to the manager

Challenges

Completely candid surveys on a manager's performance posed a few challenges that were uncovered during customer development research. Some common FUDS (fears, uncertainties, doubts) that surfaced were:

  • managers need to trust the platform with their team memeber's emails (to provide the Manager Score survey invitation)
  • team members have to be made aware that the feedback they provide are 100% anonymous
  • managers want to know what questions are being asked on the survey

User testing

To validate our designs and assumptions, prior to active project development, we enlisted the help of one of Lighthouse's long-time users to alpha test the product.

Findings from initial (and very manual) tests provided terrific insights on how managers & their teams wanted to interact with the product. Unplanned features were even built, resulting directly from UX research.

Results

Manager Score was well received and kept gaining momentum. From a chain of fitness studios, to tech consulting firms, and Silicon Valley startups - managers, leaders, and teams spanning many professions use Manager Score to gather insights on management performance. Some have even run quarterly scores to track improvement!

directing leaders Skip-level Meetings Building on top of Lighthouse's 1 on 1 meeting platform: managers meet with their team members : team members meet with their managers, introduce a feature enabling executives, directors, VPs, and managers of managers to meet with team members.

Position
Product Designer | Front-end Engineer
Team
3 people
When
Q1 ~ Q2 2017
Time
4 months

Initial discovery

We dove deep into Lighthouse's 2016 Q4 Product Survey in order to understand what had to be designed & built for Q1 2017 and validate base assumptions of what skip-level meetings would work:

  1. users need to meet with employees of their reporting managers regularly
  2. skip-level 1 on 1s need to have the same meeting navigation UI
  3. all meeting notes, action items, and agendas from resulting skip-level meetings need to be surfaced in regular 1 on 1s

Challenges

Reading survey answers and running UX interviews with current Lighthouse customers surfaced quite a few insights and possible challenges in the design process:

  • directors, vps, and other managers of managers have varying frequencies of 1 on 1 meetings - most are not consistent
  • privacy and information integrity is exceptionally important, and need to be addressed for users gain confidence in the product
  • the skip-level solution needs to be easily navigable between different meeting types, and allow users to control meeting items & frequencies without breaking the context of their meeting

Results and metrics

After addressing some key concerns that Lighthouse's users had about using Skip-levels, the feature launched early April of 2017 and the reception was resoundingly positive. Overcoming hurdles by:

  • providing language and iconography that communicates to the user that meeting notes are fully secure and private
  • designing the feature in a way that contextually preserves the content of both regular and skip-level meetings
  • actively listening, testing, and iterating on solutions with the users and their workflow

Directors, vps, and managers of managers using the platform were delighted, and Lighthouse increased in company-wide monthly expansions by 47.13%!
